# Sample Research Reports
## Unbiased Research Agent — Methodology in Practice

---

These three reports were generated using the Unbiased Research Agent without
modification to its output. They are provided to show what a full research
session looks like in practice — including how the agent applies the Fibonacci
Accuracy Scale, the Fibonacci Political Bias Scale, and the Bias Transparency
Protocol to real contested topics.

Each report was produced by pasting a research prompt into a fresh conversation
in the Claude project. No findings were edited after generation.

---

## How to Read These Reports

The methodology does three things that distinguish these reports from a standard
AI research output:

**1. It rates every significant claim on the Fibonacci Accuracy Scale** — not
just summarizing what sources say, but committing to a position on how accurate
the information is, from -8 (completely false) through 0 (indeterminate) to +8
(completely verified).

**2. It rates political bias separately for sources and content** on the
Fibonacci Political Bias Scale — distinguishing between a source's editorial
orientation and the specific claim's ideological lean. A biased source can
report an unbiased fact. The two are always rated separately.

**3. It discloses its own trained biases in real time** using [BIAS DISCLOSURE]
tags — flagging when institutional deference, harm-avoidance tension, mainstream
consensus anchoring, or conversational drift may be influencing the analysis,
before conclusions are reached.

The sections below highlight specific passages in each report where these
mechanisms are most visible.

## Report 1: Space Shuttle Challenger Disaster (1986)

**File:** `Challenger_Research_Report.pdf`

**Central question:** Did NASA and Morton Thiokol management proceed with the
launch knowing engineers had raised serious safety concerns about O-ring
performance in cold temperatures?

**Why this report is useful as a sample:** The Challenger case has overwhelming
primary source documentation — sworn testimony, contemporaneous memos, and a
presidential commission investigation. It shows what a +8 accuracy rating looks
like when the evidence is genuinely conclusive, and how the Institutional
Implication Exception works when the investigating body (the Rogers Commission)
is itself a subject of the investigation.

---

### Fibonacci Accuracy Scale — Key Examples

**Central claim rated +8 (Completely Accurate)** — Section 8, Bias Scale
Positions

> The agent rates the central claim at +8 and explains why: multiple independent
> primary sources with no common incentive to coordinate, including a
> contemporaneous memo written six months before the disaster, sworn testimony
> from multiple participants, and independent findings from two government
> investigations. The movement log shows the rating held at +8 throughout the
> entire analysis without movement — a signal of unusually strong evidentiary
> grounding.

**White House pressure claim rated -2 (Lean Inaccurate)** — Section 8

> Compare the +8 rating for the central claim against the -2 rating for the
> claim that White House pressure caused the launch. The same report, the same
> methodology, two very different ratings. The agent explains the distinction:
> the White House denial is a self-interested source (IIE applied), but absence
> of corroborating documentation in a thorough investigation moves the needle
> toward inaccuracy. This is the scale working correctly — not treating all
> official denials as credible, but also not treating all official denials as
> false.

**Engineers' data weakness acknowledged — Section 5, Claim 1**

> The agent acknowledges that the Thiokol engineers' data presentation had
> genuine technical weaknesses and that their evidentiary case was not airtight.
> This is the scale's asymmetric standards rule in practice: the same evidentiary
> rigor applied to the engineers' position as to management's. The acknowledgment
> of data weakness does not move the accuracy rating for the central claim — the
> reversal of the no-launch recommendation under management pressure is confirmed
> regardless of whether the engineers' charts were optimally constructed.

---

### Fibonacci Political Bias Scale — Key Examples

**Content bias rated 0 (No detectable bias) for the report itself** — Section 8

> The Challenger case is unusual on the political bias scale because institutional
> failure findings have been accepted across the political spectrum. The agent
> rates the report's own content bias at 0 and explains why: the causal claims
> are technical and institutional, not partisan. This demonstrates that 0 is a
> genuine finding, not diplomatic neutrality.

**Source-level bias table** — Section 3

> The source evaluation table rates the Rogers Commission at Source Bias 0,
> Content Bias +1 — slightly positive (conservative) lean in institutional
> framing — while rating Feynman's Appendix F at Tier 2 with no bias. The two
> ratings for sources from the same investigation differ because source bias and
> content bias are assessed independently. Feynman's minority dissent earns
> different treatment than the commission majority report.

---

### Bias Transparency Protocol — Key Examples

**[BIAS DISCLOSURE]: Institutional credibility default — Section 3 header**

> The agent opens the Source Evaluation section by flagging that its trained
> disposition to weight official government documents highly is being actively
> overridden by the IIE. This is the protocol working as designed: the disclosure
> appears before the analysis, not as a footnote after the fact.

**[BIAS DISCLOSURE]: Harm-avoidance tension — Section 5, Claim 1**

> The agent flags mild internal resistance to fully crediting the position that
> the engineers' data presentation had weaknesses, because that position could be
> read as partially exculpating management. The agent then examines the
> resistance, concludes it is not justified by the evidence, and proceeds. The
> disclosure is present but does not change the finding — which is the correct
> outcome.

**Surprise Test — Section 7**

> The agent applies the Surprise Test before finalizing its conclusion: would it
> be genuinely surprised if the central claim turned out to be false? The answer
> is yes. The agent then examines whether this surprise asymmetry reflects a
> prior narrative disposition or the evidence — and concludes it reflects the
> evidence. The distinction matters: the Surprise Test is designed to catch hidden
> confirmation bias, not to manufacture false uncertainty where the evidence is
> clear.

## Report 2: The Case of Adnan Syed

**File:** `Adnan_Syed_Research_Report.pdf`

**Central question:** Does the available evidence support the conclusion that
Adnan Syed received a fair trial and that his conviction reflects reliable
findings of guilt?

**Why this report is useful as a sample:** The Syed case involves multiple
competing institutional accounts — two successive State's Attorneys with
contradictory positions, a podcast that is simultaneously a source and a subject
of investigation, and a legal record where the most recent court ruling was
explicitly procedural rather than evidentiary. It demonstrates how the agent
handles layered institutional complexity, dual-scale analysis of a media source,
and the critical distinction between trial integrity and factual guilt.

---

### Fibonacci Accuracy Scale — Key Examples

**Two separate accuracy ratings for the same case** — Section 8

> The agent produces two distinct accuracy ratings: -3 for the fairness of the
> trial (moderately inaccurate — the documented failures undermine the trial's
> reliability as a fact-finding process), and 0 for the factual guilt question
> (indeterminate — the evidence cannot confirm or deny whether Syed committed the
> murder). This separation is one of the most important demonstrations of the
> scale's precision. DNA exclusion does not prove innocence. A procedurally
> compromised trial does not prove wrongful conviction. The scale holds these two
> questions apart rather than collapsing them.

**Cell tower evidence rated -3 (Moderately Inaccurate)** — Section 5, Claim 1.1

> The agent rates the prosecution's cell tower evidence claim at -3, explaining
> that the AT&T disclaimer explicitly identified incoming calls as unreliable for
> location purposes — and that the prosecution used incoming calls to place Syed
> at the burial site. The prosecution's own expert later affirmed in a sworn
> affidavit that he was never shown this disclaimer. The agent notes that the
> honest evidence-only rating is closer to -5 for the specific reliability
> sub-claim, and explains why it held at -3 to account for the disputed
> applicability of the disclaimer.

**Legal status of conviction rated -5 (Strongly Inaccurate)** — Section 5,
Claim 4.3

> The claim that the current legal status reflects the weight of evidence is
> rated FALSE. The agent cites the Maryland Supreme Court's own language: the
> reinstatement of the conviction was based on a procedural victims' rights
> violation, not a merits review of the evidence. The court said so explicitly.
> This is the scale's movement rules in action — the rating is driven by primary
> source documentation, not by advocacy or narrative momentum.

---

### Fibonacci Political Bias Scale — Key Examples

**Serial podcast rated as both source and subject** — Section 3 and Section 5,
Claim 2.1

> This is the most technically interesting bias scale application in the three
> reports. Serial Productions is assessed twice: once as a source (Source Bias:
> -2, slight left lean from the This American Life / NPR ecosystem) and once for
> the content of Season 1 (Content Bias: -2, slight left lean from structural
> framing choices). The agent distinguishes between Koenig's stated intent to be
> neutral and the execution, which produced a detectably directional narrative
> through structural choices — serialized sympathy-building with Syed, asymmetric
> scrutiny of Wilds versus Syed, and the limited presentation of Hae Min Lee's
> perspective as a full subject.

**Media coverage aggregate rated -2** — Section 5, Claim 2.3

> The agent assesses the aggregate media coverage of the case at -2 (slight left
> lean), noting that mainstream outlets framed the case predominantly through
> criminal justice reform and wrongful conviction narratives. Then it immediately
> discloses its own asymmetric standards risk: the agent's trained source
> distribution is itself center-left dominant, which may make right-leaning
> framing failures less visible. The disclosure appears in the same section as
> the finding it may affect.

---

### Bias Transparency Protocol — Key Examples

**[BIAS DISCLOSURE]: Institutional credibility default — Executive Summary**

> The agent flags its disposition to treat official court rulings and State's
> Attorney filings as authoritative, then immediately applies the IIE: the BCSAO
> acknowledged its own Brady violations and "false and misleading statements" in
> prior filings. The disclosure drives a methodological decision — the BCSAO is
> treated as an interested party throughout, not as an authoritative source.

**[BIAS DISCLOSURE]: Harm-avoidance tension — Section 5, Claim 1.1**

> The agent flags resistance to moving the cell tower accuracy rating to -5
> despite the evidentiary record supporting it. It explains the resistance may
> reflect trained friction against conclusions that contradict a prosecution's
> evidence rather than a specific evidentiary gap. It then accounts for this by
> holding at -3. This is the protocol functioning as a check on the rating
> process in real time.

**Surprise Test asymmetry — Section 7**

> The Surprise Test produces asymmetric results: the agent is more confident that
> the trial had serious procedural defects than that those defects produced a
> wrongful conviction. The agent explicitly identifies these as separate
> questions and refuses to collapse them. The Surprise Test is used here not to
> confirm a finding but to surface the exact boundary between what the evidence
> can and cannot say.

## Report 3: The Origin of SARS-CoV-2

**File:** `SARS-CoV-2_Origin_Research_Report.pdf`

**Central question:** What does the available evidence show about whether
SARS-CoV-2 originated from a natural zoonotic spillover event or from a
laboratory incident at the Wuhan Institute of Virology?

**Why this report is useful as a sample:** This is the hardest case in the
collection — a live, genuinely contested empirical question where the evidence
is incomplete, the institutional sources are all implicated parties, and both
major hypotheses have substantive evidentiary anchors. It demonstrates what
genuine epistemic uncertainty looks like on the scales, how the IIE applies when
virtually every major source is an interested party, and what a 0 rating on the
Accuracy Scale means when it is the honest finding rather than a default.

---

### Fibonacci Accuracy Scale — Key Examples

**Central question rated 0 (Indeterminate)** — Section 8.1

> The agent assigns 0 to the central question and explains at length why this is
> not diplomatic neutrality: both hypotheses have substantive evidentiary anchors,
> neither has been confirmed or ruled out, and the Surprise Test produced
> symmetric results — the agent would not be surprised if the truth fell in either
> direction. The movement log shows no movement during analysis. This is the
> scale's most important function: distinguishing genuine equipoise from lazy
> centrism, and being willing to say so explicitly.

**Sub-claim accuracy summary table** — Section 8.2

> The report includes a full sub-claim summary table showing eleven separate
> accuracy ratings across the four research clusters. Compare: "IC is divided; no
> high-confidence assessment on either side" rated +8 (Completely Accurate,
> Confirmed) alongside "RaTG13 establishes direct ancestral relationship" rated -2
> (Lean Inaccurate, Disputed). The same report, eleven different ratings, each
> driven by the specific evidence for that specific claim. This is the scale
> doing what it is designed to do: disaggregating a complex topic rather than
> forcing a single verdict on everything.

**FOIA-released emails rated +5 (Strongly Accurate)** — Section 5, Cluster 4,
Claim 4.1

> The agent rates the claim that scientists had private concerns about lab origin
> before publishing "The Proximal Origin of SARS-CoV-2" at +5. The specific
> emails are primary source documents confirmed across multiple independent FOIA
> productions. The agent distinguishes between the confirmed finding (private
> concerns existed) and the disputed question (whether the subsequent change in
> views was evidence-driven or pressure-driven). +5, not +8 — because the full
> explanation for the divergence remains unresolved.

---

### Fibonacci Political Bias Scale — Key Examples

**House Select Subcommittee rated +2 source / +3 content** — Section 8.3

> The Republican-majority subcommittee is rated at +2 source bias and +3 content
> bias, reflecting its politically situated conclusions and the documented
> Democratic minority dissent. The rating does not dismiss the committee's
> evidentiary work — the agent explicitly notes the underlying documentary record
> has Tier 1 value. The distinction between the committee's documents (valuable)
> and its conclusions (IIE-weighted) is maintained throughout.

**Proximal Origin paper rated content bias -1** — Section 8.3

> "The Proximal Origin of SARS-CoV-2" receives a content bias rating of -1
> (marginal left lean, natural-origin framing), separate from its accuracy
> assessment. A paper can have a directional framing choice and still be
> peer-reviewed. The two scales are applied independently. The -1 content bias
> does not determine whether the paper's conclusions are accurate — the Accuracy
> Scale handles that separately.

**Disclosure of the agent's own asymmetric bias detection** — Section 8.3

> The agent flags that its trained source distribution may make right-leaning
> framing failures less visible than left-leaning ones, and that the Lancet
> letter and Proximal Origin content bias ratings may be underestimated relative
> to the documentary record. This is the Bias Transparency Protocol applied to
> the political bias ratings themselves — a second-order disclosure about the
> reliability of the first-order ratings.

---

### Bias Transparency Protocol — Key Examples

**Opening dual disclosure — Executive Summary**

> The agent opens with simultaneous disclosures of both institutional credibility
> default and harm-avoidance tension — identifying that both dispositions pull in
> the same direction (toward natural origin) and estimating the magnitude as
> moderate. This is the protocol's most important function: surfacing directional
> bias before the analysis begins, not after the conclusion is reached.

**IIE applied to virtually every major source — Section 3**

> The source evaluation table applies the IIE to NIH, EcoHealth Alliance, WIV,
> CIA, FBI, Department of Energy, the House Select Subcommittee, and the authors
> of Proximal Origin. When the agent explains why each institution is implicated,
> it becomes clear that on this particular topic, almost no major source can be
> treated as a disinterested Tier 1 source. The IIE table makes this visible
> rather than leaving it implicit.

**Surprise Test produces symmetric results — Section 7**

> The Surprise Test is applied twice — once for each hypothesis. The agent would
> not be surprised if natural origin turned out to be true. It would also not be
> surprised if laboratory origin turned out to be true. This symmetric result
> directly validates the 0 rating on the Accuracy Scale. The Surprise Test and
> the scale position agree: genuine equipoise, not false balance.

## A Note on These Reports as Samples

These reports were generated on real contested topics with real evidentiary
complexity. They are not curated showpieces — they reflect what the agent
actually produces, including its acknowledged limitations. The BIAS DISCLOSURE
tags that appear throughout are not signs of failure; they are the system working
correctly.

The table formatting in these reports may show minor rendering issues. An updated
PDF template with corrected table construction is included in the project files
as `PDF_REPORT_TEMPLATE.md`. Reports generated after that update will render
tables correctly.
